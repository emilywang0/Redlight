Take Action: Resources To Help

    1) Learn the signs and report any suspicious activity.

  • Learn where your local organizations are that can best support any situations dealing with human trafficking.
  • Thorn and Polaris are both companies working together and developed a textline that can be accessed by texting BeFree to 233733.
  • Register for trainings to study signs from a professional.

    • 2) Be a responsible consumer by checking if your purchases use human trafficking in the process.

  • This interactive website displays how to best protect your brand from forced labor and understanding the risk that comes from it.
    Link: https://www.responsiblesourcingtool.org
  • The U.S. Bureau of International Affair's List shows which products of different countries exploit labor in their materials.
    Link: https://www.dol.gov/agencies/ilab/reports/child-labor/list-of-goods

    • 3) Educate yourself and others on this important subject.

  • Be aware of current and upcoming news on human trafficking.
  • Share articles and well-sourced writings on social media.
  • Support your news media to continually to write about human trafficking and its harms.

    • 4) Be involved in fighting for change.

  • Write a letter/email to your representatives and sign petitions.
  • Find local organizations to volunteer and support to spread the word.
  • Run fundraisers for organizations fighting against human trafficking.
